# Technology Infrastructure for Distributed Learning Environments

## Platform Selection

The LMS is the center of gravity for distributed learning, but selecting one requires looking beyond feature lists. Evaluate integration capabilities with existing SIS, authentication systems, and third-party tools. Assess accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA minimum). Consider the vendor's track record with institutions of your size and complexity. Most importantly, evaluate the total ecosystem -- an LMS with fewer native features but strong APIs and an active integration marketplace may serve you better than a monolithic platform that does everything adequately and nothing well.

## Content Design for Remote Delivery

Content designed for in-person delivery rarely translates directly to remote formats. Lecture recordings should be chunked into 10-15 minute segments with embedded knowledge checks rather than posted as hour-long recordings. Supplement video with written summaries, downloadable resources, and interactive elements. Design with mobile access in mind -- a significant percentage of students access course materials primarily through phones, and content that requires a desktop browser excludes them.

## Faculty Development

Faculty are not automatically effective remote instructors because they are effective classroom instructors. The skills are related but distinct. Invest in instructional design support that goes beyond technology training. Pair faculty with instructional designers who can help them reimagine course structure, assessment methods, and engagement strategies for distributed environments. Create communities of practice where faculty share what's working and troubleshoot challenges together.

## Quality Assurance

Establish clear quality standards for online and hybrid courses. Use rubrics like Quality Matters or the OLC Scorecard to evaluate course design. Conduct regular reviews and provide constructive feedback. Track student outcomes data disaggregated by modality to identify quality gaps between in-person and remote delivery. Use these findings to target professional development and resource allocation where they'll have the most impact.
